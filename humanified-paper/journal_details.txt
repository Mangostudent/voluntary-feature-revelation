1. JOURNAL OVERVIEW & REPUTATION
---------------------------------
* Journal Name: ACM Transactions on Intelligent Systems and Technology (TIST)
* Scope: Interdisciplinary venue focusing on intelligent systems, machine learning, 
         data mining, human-computer interaction, intelligent control, and AI.
* Reputation: Highly prestigious, Q1-ranked journal in Artificial Intelligence and 
              Intelligent Systems.
* Metrics: Recent Impact Factor (IF): ~6.6 - 7.6
           CiteScore: ~11.2 - 11.7
           SJR (SCImago Journal Rank): ~1.36
* Significance: Perfect fit for "Voluntary Feature Revelation" due to its focus 
                 on strategic aspects of machine learning, game-theoretic agent 
                 behavior, and practical algorithms with solid guarantees.

2. SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS & FORMATTING
---------------------------------------
* Template: Official ACM Primary Article Template (acmart.cls).
* Format Option: \documentclass[acmsmall]{acmart} (Single-column layout).
* Bibliography Style: ACM-Reference-Format (linked to a references.bib file).
* Page Limits: No strict page limit for journal submissions. TIST encourages 
               thorough proofs, detailed appendices, and exhaustive simulation 
               evaluations. Average length is 25-40 pages.
* Abstract Length: 150 to 250 words, written in a single paragraph.
* Special Sections: Mandatory ACM Computing Classification System (CCS) concepts 
                     for camera-ready. Author acknowledgments must use the "acks" 
                     environment.

3. TIMELINES & TURNAROUND
--------------------------
* Review Process: Traditional peer-review by 3 independent expert reviewers.
* Review Cycle: Rigorous peer-review standards. Average time to first decision is 
                typically 3 to 6 months (90-180 days), but final publication 
                decisions can take 6 to 9 months depending on revision cycles.

4. ACM GENERATIVE AI POLICY & AUTHOR GUIDELINES
-----------------------------------------------
* AI Cannot Be an Author: Generative AI tools (LLMs) cannot be listed as co-authors. 
  Authorship is strictly reserved for human contributors.
* Full Disclosure: The use of generative AI tools to assist in writing drafts 
  (such as proof structures, text polishing, or code outlines) must be fully 
  disclosed in the manuscript. Typically, this disclosure is placed in the 
  Acknowledgments section (the "acks" environment in acmart).
* Author Responsibility: Human authors remain 100% accountable for the accuracy, 
  originality, and integrity of all content.
* Reviewer Policy: Reviewers are strictly prohibited from uploading confidential 
  submissions into third-party AI tools (such as LLMs) that do not guarantee data 
  confidentiality.
