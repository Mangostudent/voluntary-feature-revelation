ACM Transactions on Economics and Computation (TEAC) - Journal Submission Info
================================================================================

1. JOURNAL OVERVIEW & REPUTATION
---------------------------------
* Journal Name: ACM Transactions on Economics and Computation (TEAC)
* Scope: Interdisciplinary venue for computer science (machine learning, multi-
         agent systems) and economics (game theory, mechanism design, auctions).
* CORE Rank: B / A boundary (a highly prestigious, niche journal; the premier 
             journal venue for the ACM EC community).
* Indexing: Indexed in Scopus, SCIE, Google Scholar, DBLP. SCImago Q2 rating.
* Significance: Focuses on algorithmic game theory, learning in games, and 
                strategic ML. Highly relevant to "Strategic Feature Withholding".

2. SUBMISSION REQUIREMENTS & FORMATTING
---------------------------------------
* Template: Official ACM Primary Article Template (acmart.cls).
* Format Option: \documentclass[acmsmall]{acmart} (Single-column layout).
* Bibliography Style: ACM-Reference-Format (linked to a references.bib file).
* Page Limits: No strict page limit for journal submissions. Unlike conferences,
              TEAC encourages thorough proofs, detailed appendices, and 
              exhaustive experiments. Average length is 20-35 pages.
* Abstract Length: 150 to 250 words, written in a single paragraph.
* Special Sections: Mandatory ACM Computing Classification System (CCS) concepts
                    for camera-ready. Author acknowledgments must use the "acks"
                    environment.

3. TIMELINES & TURNAROUND
--------------------------
* Review Process: Traditional peer-review by 3 independent expert reviewers.
* Average Time to First Decision: 3 to 6 months (90-180 days).
* Fast Track Option: "Forward-to-Journal" program available for papers that
                     were submitted to/presented at the ACM EC conference,
                     utilizing previous reviews to speed up the decision.

5. ACM GENERATIVE AI POLICY & AUTHOR GUIDELINES
-----------------------------------------------
* AI Cannot Be an Author: Generative AI tools (like ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) cannot 
  be listed as co-authors on any ACM publication. Authorship is strictly reserved 
  for human contributors.
* Full Disclosure: If Generative AI tools are used to generate content (text, 
  images, mathematical derivations, or code), this usage must be fully disclosed 
  in the manuscript. Typically, this disclosure is placed in the Acknowledgments 
  section (the "acks" environment in acmart).
* Exception for Writing Helpers: Standard word processors, spelling checkers, 
  and grammar check tools (minor stylistic polishing) do not require disclosure.
* Author Responsibility: The human authors remain 100% accountable for the 
  accuracy, originality, and integrity of all content (including ensuring that 
  AI-generated citations, math proofs, or code are correct and free from plagiarism).
